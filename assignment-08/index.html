Materials:

The meterials needed to create this custom project are as follows:

The Hot Wheels 1995 Mazda Miata toy car Required
Drill (both hand or electric drills will work) Required
0.5mm drill bit Required
Paint stripper Required
Scotch-Bright pad optional
2000 grit sand paper Required
"Helping hands" optional
Red spray paint Required
Clear coat spray optional
Rubber wheels optional

Step 1: Prep

  1. Disassemble the car by drilling out the two pins underneath the car.
  2. The body windshield interior and base will come apart in separate pieces.
  3. Place metal body in a small cup of paint stripper and let it sit for 2 hours.
  4. Carefully remove the body and wash it off in a sink.
  5. I used a Scotch brite on the metal to help shine it up a bit, but this is not required.

Step 2: Paint

  1. Find something to hold the car such as a box or a set of Helping hands for painting.
  2. Spray the entire body with multiple coats of paint
  3. Let the paint completely dry for 48 hours
  4. Spray clear coat on top of the paint for a more glossy finish

Step 3: Finish

  1. Once you are satisfied with the paint job, start assembling the interior parts back together
  2. You may replace the plastic wheels with rubber wheels like I have done, but this is optional
  3. Because I am making a replica of real car, I have used sandpaper to roughen the body
  4. Put all the parts back together and add super glue to the pin holes on the bottom of the base
  5. It is now complete!
